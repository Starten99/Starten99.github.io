Gastronomic brands of Tula
region

The Tula region presented its gastronomic brands in a "clear glade" within the framework of the federal project "Gastronomic Map of Russia".
Gastronomic brands of the Tula region were presented on August 17, 2017 in the territory of the museum-estate of L.N. Tolstoy to the participants of the expedition "South" of the federal project "The Gastronomic Map of Russia".
The Tula region presented the "People's Table" in the concept of "Tula Tea Party" with the main symbols known all over the country and abroad - Tula samovars, Tula gingerbread, Belevian pastille, sugar heads. A special treat at the feast was a dish prepared according to the recipes of the writer's wife Sophia Tolstoy - An Ankovsky Pie (or Anka Pie), as well as old dishes from the Tula cuisine - the Buninsky Garden cake and pies from the nettle. Young, but already popular gastronomic brands - Suvorov sweets, birch kvass, Venovki rolls, Belevsky marmalade, Belevian marshmallow, berry tea and much more were also represented on the "public table" of the Tula region.
The leader of the expedition "South" of the federal project "Gastronomic Map of Russia" Ekaterina Shapovalova noted that the Tula region is rich in historical and unique gastronomic brands, many of which have significant export potential.
